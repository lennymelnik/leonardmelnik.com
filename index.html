Darknet, Digital Forensics, Stolen Credentials

Exploring the use of Stolen Credentials in Digital Forensics

This paper delves into the realm of digital forensics, focusing on the critical issue of stolen credentials. It outlines the methods by which credentials are compromised, including database breaches, malware, and phishing, and discusses how digital forensics and law enforcement can acquire these credentials for investigation. The paper highlights the benefits and challenges of using stolen credentials in forensics, such as aiding in identifying suspects and accessing encrypted files, while also acknowledging the complications they introduce, like impersonation and altered evidence. The conclusion emphasizes the pivotal role of credentials in digital investigations and the necessity of securing them against theft.

Data Breaches/CyberThreats Faced by Small Businesses and How Threat Intelligence can Help

In this paper, I explore the vulnerability of small businesses to cyber threats and the importance of threat intelligence in mitigating these risks. I define small businesses and threat intelligence, discuss various attack vectors like phishing and compromised credentials, and the devastating impact of data breaches on small enterprises.
